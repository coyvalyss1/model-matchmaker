## r/GenX Post (Discussion, not product)

**Title:**
How do you figure out what matters when the routine is on autopilot?

**Body:**
I'm in my late 40s. Kids are in middle/high school, career is solid but doesn't excite me anymore, weekends blur together. I know I should have goals but honestly I don't know what they'd be.

The career ladder thing stopped mattering somewhere along the way. The stuff that used to drive me (get the next promotion, buy the house, get the kids into good schools) is either done or feels hollow now.

I'm not depressed. I'm just... on autopilot. And I don't know what I'm supposed to want next.

Everyone says "figure out what matters to you" but that's not a plan, that's just the question rephrased.

I kept waiting for the answer to appear. It didn't. So I decided to build the thing I wished existed: not a productivity app, not a journal, something that actually helps you figure out what's next when you're stuck in the routine.

(I'll drop a link if people are curious, but mostly I'm just wondering: what works for you when you're trying to figure out what actually matters? How do other people handle this mid-life "now what?" moment?)
